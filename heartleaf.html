Quick Care

Light

Heartleaf Philidendrons are pretty leniate when it comes to like, they can handle bright indirect light to sometimes low indirect light. They also can withstand some direct sunlight, but be careful with the amount because they can get burned.

Water

Water every 1 to two weeks as the pot mostly dries out. But if you forget, dont worry this plant is hardy and will wilt when it REALLY needs some water.

Toxicity to Pets

Yes, if ingensted call your vet immedietly. Luckily this plant tends to be kept out of their reach.

Philidendron

Heartleaf

The Heartleaf Philidendron is a great plant for all plant parents. It is generally easy and fast grower, adding a nice greenery to any space.

Care

Generally the Heartleaf Philidendron is easy to care for. They like bright indriect light, but can tolderate down to low light (though they might grow leggy), and they can tolerant some direct some but you should be careful because they can get sunburened with too much direct sun. They like to be watered every 1 to 2 weeks like most plants, do well in average home temperatures and humidity, and like well draining soil. Since they are a climbing plant you can give them something to climb and you might get bigger leaves.

Common Problems

This is generally a very easy-going plant. SYMPTOM: Leaves turn yellow and drop CAUSE: Overwatered SYMPTOM: Wilting CAUSE: Under watered SYMPTOM: Soft or black stems CAUSE: Rot or root disease
