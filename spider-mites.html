Problem

Webbing on leaves

In this case there are two things this could be. A nice little spider liked your plant and wated to decorate it or, the worse option, spide rmites. I abosilutly hate these little guys, they are destructive and so gross. Also hard to get rid of. But below are some of the ways I have delt with them in the past and how I prevent them from every being near me again.

Preventative.

If you EVER see webbing on a plant at a store, even it is the most amazing plant you have seen, just don't buy it. HYou do not want to deal with spider mites. Also the help them not sprouting out of nothing, which them seem to like to do, I spray my plants whenever I think of it with a soap mixture that kills the spdier mites on impact. To make it mix a large amount of water to like 1 table spoon castile soap (i use lavendar sented). This keeps them away from me.

Crying

Lets say you ignored my warning and brought back a plant with webbing anyway or just did it cause you didn't know, here is how you can try to save it. Fistly, get it FAR away from any of your plants. If you dont have any plants in your abthroom, keep it in there with the door closed. Stick it in the bath and hose that thing off, really scrub. Spray it with the sop mixture i talked about abovre and WIPE each leaf and stem, really get in there these things can hide. Then rise off again and spray with the saop mixture again. Let it sit for a little bit in the bathroom and keep and eye on it. Put some uv lights and there and leave the plant for a week. Then repeat the rise and soap process you did before. Leave for another week and check it. If you dont see anymore webbing, congrats you might have solved the prblem. You can reintroduce it into your other plants but keep an eye on everythign I would say for a month, just in case. Good luck!
