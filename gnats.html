Problem

Small Black Bugs

you're relaxing on your couch when all of a sudden you start getting bothered by little black bugs. These bugs are likely fungus gnats. Little black bugs that LOVE overwatered plants and hatch in the soil and crawl out. I hate to say it, but in every plant parent journey you probably will have a bad case of fungus gnats at least once. But there are a few ways you can go about fighting them.

Sticky Traps

Once you realize you have fungus gnats my reccomendation would be to put some stick traps around your plants. I personally like the kind that you can stake right in the pot, and ideally you can put them right at the plants that are the soruce of the problem. This can help cut back on the number greatly and help them keep the gnats more in check.

Preventative or COmbative

Your fungus gnats likely came home with a new plant and have decided now is the time to appear. One thing that can kill the larva in the soil is hydrogeneroxide. I create a mixture that is about one part hydrogenperoxide to one part water and pour it in my new plants. This helps kill any larva or other pests in the soil. It also adds air to the soil for the plant. But be careful with this method, some plants may respond badly and it kills any beneficial things in the soil as well.

Let it Dry

Another method you can do if the bugs are just too much, which they always are those stinkers, is to just not water your plants. Fungus gnats need wet soil to reproduce, so if you let your soil dry out for around 2 weeks or so, this should help the problem GREATLY. Sometimes you have to take this method to really get them gone, though your plants might hate it. Keep an eye on your plants if you try this.

Kill them as they Emerge

Another method that I have heard about is some kind of sand or diotematious earth. You place the substance on the surface of the soil and it shreds the gnats a sthey emerge, this, though agressive sounding, I heard works well.
