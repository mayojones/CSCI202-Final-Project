Quick Care

Light

Monsteras do best with bright to medium indirect sunlight. But they can withstand some direct sunlight in mornigs or evens. Avoid direct, afternoon light.

Water

Water every 1-2 weeks, allowing soil to dry out between waterings. Expect to water more often in brighter light and less often in lower light. Sometimes their leaves will curl in when they need water.

Toxicity to Pets

Technically yes, this is toxic to pets, but also not really. It is only if the pet bites the plant, and the tympical symptoms is a mild, painful reaction. Generally this plant can be considered safe around none biters!

Monstera Deliciosa

Monsteras are a great plant for begginger planty people all the way to experts. These plants not only add a wonderfuly and tropical greenery to a space, but they are especially rewarding plant. My reccomendation is the buy a young monstera and watch as it turns into a big and beautiful statement piece. These plants can get quite large, so get ready for them to take up space!

Care

Generally the Monstera is an easy to care for, hardy plant. Great for platy noivces or experts. Their care is pretty typical, they of course like bright indirect sun and like being watered every 1 to 2 weeks (when their soil drys out mostly). They will be fine with normal room humidity and prefer temps in the 65°F-85°F range (18°C-30°C). It’s best not to let it go below 60°F (15°C). Like most plants they like fast draining soil that allows them to not sit in standing water. Additionally, since they are a climbing plant they like having something to lean on, I currently have my monstera supported with a wooden dowel.

Common Problems

The Monstera is an easy-going plant and is generally pest-free. Treat pests as soon as they appear with weekly sprays of a natural pesticide like neem oil and regular wipe-downs of the plant.
