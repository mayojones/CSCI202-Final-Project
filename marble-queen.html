Quick Care

Light

Pothos plants thrive in bright, indirect light, but they can tolerate medium to low indirect light, for instance, in north-facing homes or during cooler seasons with shorter days. This plant is not suited for intense, direct sun, which will burn and brown its leaves.

Water

Water your Pothos every 1-2 weeks, allowing soil to dry out between waterings. Expect to water more often in brighter light and less often in lower light.

Toxicity to Pets

Yes, Pothos can be irritating to cats, dogs, and humans if foliage is consumed. Luckily this plant tends to be kept out of their reach.

Care

The pothos has the reputation of being one of the easiest houseplants to take care of. Its care needs are basically the same as most house plants: Bright indirect light, water when soil mostly dry, and fast draining soil. The fun thin about this plant and other pothos is that they are natural climbers. This means that is you give them something to climb up they can grow massive leaves the size of giant monstera leaves. A very cool thing indeed!

Common Problems
