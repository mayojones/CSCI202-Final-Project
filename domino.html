Quick Care

Light

Variegated peace lily ‘Domino’ plants require bright to moderate indirect light. They also benefit from some partial shade during the afternoon. An east-facing window is an ideal location because it provides bright sunlight that isn’t as intense as the afternoon sun. They can handle low light in some situations, but move them if they start growing leggy.

Water

Water every 1 -2 weeks depending on how dry the soil is. But be warned if the Peace lily decides it needs water it will tell you by simply wilting to the point they look like they are dying. Don't worry, if you give them some water they should be totally fine by the next day or even in a few hours.

Toxicity to Pets

Unfortunately, a variegated peace lily ‘Domino’ is toxic to both humans and pets.

Care

Generally the Peace Lily is an easy to care for. Great for platy noivces or experts. Their care is pretty typical, while their lighting needs can be variable, they like being watered every 1 to 2 weeks (when their soil drys out mostly). They will be fine with normal room humidity and prefer temps higher than 55 degrees. Like most plants they like fast draining soil that allows them to not sit in standing water.

Common Problems

Treat pests as soon as they appear with weekly sprays of a natural pesticide like neem oil and regular wipe-downs of the plant. wilting: The two most common causes of a Peace Lily wilting are overwatering and underwatering. It seems unusual that opposite issues can cause the same problem with your plant. This makes wilting the most dangerous problem, as the wrong treatment can be devastating for your plant. test first by touching the soil to dioagnose. Brown Tips And Edges: Both overwatering and underwatering can cause brown leaf tips and edges. However, this can happen with mild underwatering, but will only happen with severe overwatering. Yellowing Leaves: The most common reason for your Peace Lily’s striking green leaves to turn yellow is overwatering. Sometimes, factors like a sudden change in temperature or location might also stress the plant, causing its leaves to turn yellow.
