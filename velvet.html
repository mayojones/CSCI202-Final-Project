Quick Care

Light

Thrives in medium to bright indirect light. Not suited for direct sun or low light. Too much direct light can fade the beautifucl color of the leaves. Alocasia can go into a dormancy phase and dieback during winter. Increasing light and humidity can help deter this.

Water

Water every 1-2 weeks, allowing soil to dry out half way down between waterings. Expect to water more often in brighter light and less often in lower light. This plant can benefit from extra humidity. Alocasia plants can be sensitive to hard tap water. Try using filtered water or leaving water out overnight before using.

Toxicity to Pets

Sadly, yes they are very poisonous to pets and humans. At this point just assume most plants are not good for your pets :(

Alocasia

Black Velvet

The Black Velvet Alocasia is one of my favorite plants that I own. She looks beautiful no matter where I put her. Though Alocasias can be fussy, the Black Velvet tends to be a little bit more low maintence than the others. WIth her beautiful leaf color and shape this plant adds an intriguing and wonder look to any home. This is a plant I would reccomend for any planty person taht is ready to step up their plant game for the basic level.

Care

WHile the Black Velvet can be a not very high maintence plant, ideally it would be. They ideally would thrive in a high humidty place with nice bright idirect light and fast draining soil. Currently mine Black Velvet is doing very well in average room humidity and filtered water every 1 -2 weeks. Love this gal.

Common Problems

Treat pests as soon as they appear with weekly sprays of a natural pesticide like neem oil and regular wipe-downs of the plant. Yellow leaves, wet potting mix: Overwatering Drooping, leaf curl, dry potting mix: Thirsty plant, underwatered Leaf drop, etiolation: Not enough light
